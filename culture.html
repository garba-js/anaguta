The History of
the Anaguta

A journey through centuries of settlement, survival, culture, and resilience on the Plateau of Nigeria.

The Beginning

A People
Born of the Plateau

The Anaguta are one of the indigenous peoples of Plateau State, Nigeria — known historically as the "people of bow and arrow" for their legendary skill in archery and warfare.

Long before the arrival of colonial boundaries, the Anaguta occupied the rugged hills and fertile valleys around what is today the city of Jos — a land they have farmed, protected, and revered for generations.

Their history is not merely a catalogue of events — it is a living story of identity, resistance, and an enduring bond with the land.

Through the Ages

A Timeline of History

Ancient Era
Pre-1500
Founding Settlements on the Plateau

The Anaguta establish permanent settlements in the hills surrounding present-day Jos. Their early society is organised around clan groups, communal farming, and a warrior tradition of defending territorial boundaries.

Traditional Era
1500s–1800s
The Age of the Bow and Arrow

Renowned as the "people of bow and arrow", the Anaguta successfully defend their plateau home against waves of incursions. Trade routes, royal structures, and rich oral traditions develop alongside a deep spiritual relationship with the land.

Colonial Period
1900s
British Colonisation & the Birth of Jos

The British colonial administration establishes Jos as a tin-mining hub, displacing many Anaguta from their ancestral lands. Despite this pressure, Anaguta cultural practices persist underground — passed from elder to child in defiance of erasure.

Post-Independence
1960s–1990s
Independence, Identity & Cultural Assertion

Following Nigerian independence, Anaguta leaders and communities begin formally asserting their indigenous rights and cultural identity. Traditional institutions are revived, and efforts to document customs, language, and oral history intensify.

Modern Era
2000s
Conflict, Resilience & Solidarity

The Jos crisis tests the community deeply, but the Anaguta demonstrate remarkable resilience. Community organisations mobilise for peace, reconstruction, and the protection of their cultural landmarks and kinship networks.

Today
2020s
Anaguta Tradomodern — Heritage Meets the Digital Age

A new generation of Anaguta takes their heritage to the world. Anaguta Tradomodern is founded — marrying ancestral pride with modern storytelling, digital archives, cultural events, and community outreach to ensure no story is ever forgotten.

"Until the lion learns to write, every story will glorify the hunter."
— African Proverb
